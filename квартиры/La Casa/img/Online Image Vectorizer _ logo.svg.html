Help: Drag and Drop

 

Help: Blur

The blur parameter defines the smoothing factor around each color patch.


Q & A
  • How to remove white (or transparent) empty spaces (or lines) around color patches?
    • Increase blur factor until all empty spaces are gone

Help: Edges

Select a edge filter to reduce to sharpen output and reduce the number of colors in an image


  • Canny (experimental): Preprocess the input image with Canny Filter
  • None: disable filtering

Help: Layers

Configure the layers (areas of same colors) for the output vector image:


Q & A
  • Ignore one color for the output?
    • Move the mouse over the color circle and a disable the checkbox
  • Too many small speckles around larger contours?
    • Move the color circle of the larger contour to the left and the color circles of the speckles to the right
  • Group similar colors automatically?
    • Use the slider or the 'tolerance' parameter
  • Remove a color from a group?
    • Drag & drop the color circle in the 'free' (gray) area of the layers panel and release the mouse button

Help: Mins

The mins parameter defines the size of speckles which will be ignored during vectorization.


Q & A
  • Too many small speckles around larger contours?
    • Increase the mins parameter

Help: Colors

Select the smallest amount of colors suitable for your image for best performance and smaller output vector file (SVG).


Q & A
  • Some colors not shown correcty?
    • Increase the colors one step at a time until all colors look OK in the left preview image

Help: Crowd Source

enable crowd source and your image will be also edited by other users of www.vectorizer.io and you will see their edits in the "variants" section.

Help: Color Grouping (Tolerance)

Tolerance defines the factor which is used to automatically merge similar colors in the layers list.


Q & A
  • How to reduce the numbers of colors in the layers list?
    • Increase the tolerance until similiar colors are merged together in groups
  • How to increase the detail level in the output image?
    • Decrease the tolerance until the output image has the detail level you want
